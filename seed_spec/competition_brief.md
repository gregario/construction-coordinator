# Construction Manager — Competition Brief

## Market Density: Contested (pro-GC market) / Blue Ocean (owner-builder niche)

## Advisory Verdict: **CLEAR LANE**

Construction management software is crowded for professional GCs. For expert owner-builders managing a single new build, the combination of **material lead times + dependency cascade + mobile daily briefing** is unoccupied.

## Reference Products

### Buildertrend — https://buildertrend.com
- **Dimension**: feature-completeness
- **What to learn**: comprehensive entity model for residential construction (stages, tasks, materials, subs, budget, change orders, documents).
- **What to reject**: pro-GC pricing ($399+/mo), enterprise UI density, 47-field forms, overwhelming feature sprawl, multi-project orientation. Built for GCs managing 20 projects, not owner-builders managing 1.

### BuildBook — https://buildbook.co
- **Dimensions**: ux-tone, simplicity
- **What to learn**: warm tone, calm UI, plain language, mobile-friendly, approachable onboarding.
- **What to reject**: client-communication orientation (we're single-user, not collaborative with a homeowner because the user IS the homeowner).

### HBApp — https://homebuilding.app
- **Dimension**: audience-framing
- **What to learn**: owner-builder positioning, plain-English copy, focus on self-build journey.
- **What to reject**: limited scheduling/cascade capability — their scheduling is static.

### TeamGantt — https://www.teamgantt.com
- **Dimension**: gantt-ux
- **What to learn**: interactive Gantt conventions — drag to reschedule, dependency arrows, zoom levels, critical path highlight.
- **What to reject**: generic PM framing (not construction-specific, no material lead times, no trade concept).

## Positioning Statement

**For expert owner-builders building one house**, Construction Manager is the only tool that **automatically recalculates your entire build — schedule, material order-by dates, trade callouts — the moment one thing slips**, so you can **stop re-walking your plan across three apps every morning**.
