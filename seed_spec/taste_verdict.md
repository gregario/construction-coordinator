# Construction Manager — Taste Verdict

## Mode: HOLD

User approved continuation without additional taste modifications: **"agreed continue"**.

## Sharpened Brief

### Tone
**Warm, calm, tactile.** The user is stressed enough by their build — the app should feel like a reliable foreman, not a dashboard. Evokes the jobsite (wood, clay, canvas) rather than the spreadsheet.

### Visual Direction
- **Palette**: warm earth tones — clay (#8B5E3C primary), sage (#87A96B), amber (#D4A355), warm off-white (#FAF7F2) background. Deep umber text.
- **Avoid**: blue-grey enterprise, purple gradients, dark-mode-first, neon accents, glassmorphism.
- **Typography**: Inter. Generous line-height. Clear hierarchy (no 12 levels).
- **Shape language**: soft rounded corners (0.5rem typical), warm soft shadows, no harsh borders.

### Interaction Temperament
- **Minimal friction for daily use** — briefing check-in should take <30 seconds.
- **Undo everywhere** — cascade changes are reversible via toast.
- **Preview before commit** — cascade impact shown before save, not as surprise.
- **Zero-surprise mobile** — thumb-reach bottom nav, 44px touch targets, pull-to-refresh.

### Voice
Second-person, plain language. "Your foundation pour shifted 3 days. 12 downstream tasks rescheduled." Not: "Schedule recalculation complete. 12 entities affected."

### Anti-Slop Commitments
- No gradient hero headers
- No emoji-as-icon
- No centered-card signup page
- No aspirational dashboard density
- No Lorem Ipsum
- No generic collaboration illustrations
- No skeleton-of-everything loading states

## Taste North Star

> It should feel like opening a well-worn notebook that somehow also re-plans your whole build.
